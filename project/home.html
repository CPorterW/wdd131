I believe in our culture

I have met many members and ex-members that hate the 'culture of the church'. I was once one of them. The most common critique is based on a tendency towards perfectionism, but perfectionism is against our doctrine, and although I once blamed the church for my parent's strictness, I've discovered that most of our people are merciful and forgiving. Our church and our Christ certainly are.

I believe in solidarity

Our enemies are unified and vocal. Why aren't we? Why is atheism and infidelity vogue? Why aren't we as passionate about our faith as they are about carnality? Our prophet is God's mouthpiece at times, and at others he is the man God thought wise enough to guide us. We should promote the policies of the church, whether or not we agreed with the position before the church joined it. When President Nelson recommended the Covid vaccine, did you set aside your pride and follow his counsel? When Jeffrey R Holland reminded us to defend the family with metaphorical muskets, did you return fire? Do you accept that polygamy was once the law of God? Our faith is the source of our unity, but only if we are willing to consecrate our hearts to God and His church. I believe that cultural pride is an aid to our solidarity, and so have collected some podcasts and books that secularly promote the spirit of the law.
