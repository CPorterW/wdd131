A passionate, (almost) exciting explanation of American history.

Greg Jackson goes out of his way to give every historical figure a fair shake. From George Washington to Theodore Roosevelt, Greg seeks to understand the hearts behind our history. He also seeks to make it interesting. From exploding heads to pop culture references, his sense of humor makes history tolerable. The best part is how Greg pulls you into the mind and body of the people experiencing history's most dramatic moments.

HTDS embodies our people.

Understanding the hearts of others is nearly the definition of charity. Understanding our heritage is a favorite pastime of our people. Rewriting the lives of slandered and exalted figures to show who they really were is a generous tribute. By combing through thousands of historical documents, Greg has been able to piece together the most inspiring moments of American history. He doesn't hold back though. He is very upfront about the sins of our fathers, but argues that we can't be fully responsible for conforming to an imperfect culture. He shows that men (and women) can be heroes. I've learned a lot about why civilizations and people do what they do. I feel proud of my ancestors. There's no better way to understand humanity than by intimately examining countless life stories through the eyes of someone who looks first at the heart.
